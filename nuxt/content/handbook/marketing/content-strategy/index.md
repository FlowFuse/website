# Content Strategy

Content is the primary way FlowFuse builds trust, attracts the right audience, and converts interest into pipeline. This page defines how we think about content across the buyer journey, and how we plan, schedule, and improve it continuously.

## Content Funnel Stages

Every piece of content we create serves a specific purpose depending on where a prospect is in their journey. We use three stages — TOFU, MOFU, and BOFU — to frame content decisions.

### TOFU — Top of Funnel (Awareness)

TOFU content reaches people who have a problem but haven't yet discovered FlowFuse as a solution. The goal is to educate, earn trust, and drive organic traffic.

**Audience:** Engineers and OT/IT managers exploring IIoT, Node-RED, or industrial automation broadly. Often at [Stage 0–2](/handbook/marketing/messaging/#prospect-stages-node-red-awareness) of Node-RED awareness.

**Characteristics:**
- Broad, educational, and problem-focused — not product-focused
- Optimized for organic search (SEO) and social sharing
- Answers questions people are already asking
- No sales pressure; value is the CTA

**Examples for FlowFuse:**
- How-to guides: "How to connect a PLC to Node-RED", "Getting started with MQTT"
- Concept explainers: "What is a Unified Namespace?", "OPC UA explained"
- Industry trend articles: "How manufacturers are approaching edge computing"
- Short social video clips demonstrating a Node-RED flow
- Podcast episodes covering industrial digitalization topics
- Infographics explaining IIoT architecture patterns

**Primary distribution:** Blog, LinkedIn, YouTube, Node-RED community channels

---

### MOFU — Middle of Funnel (Consideration)

MOFU content reaches people who understand the problem space and are evaluating solutions. The goal is to demonstrate that FlowFuse is the right choice and to capture leads.

**Audience:** Engineers and technical decision-makers comparing platforms or deepening their Node-RED use. Often at [Stage 2–3](/handbook/marketing/messaging/#prospect-stages-node-red-awareness).

**Characteristics:**
- Solution-aware and comparison-oriented
- Often gated (whitepapers, guides) or event-based (webinars)
- Demonstrates specific FlowFuse capabilities in realistic contexts
- Designed to capture contact information or deepen engagement

**Examples for FlowFuse:**
- Deep-dive tutorials: "Managing 50 Node-RED instances with FlowFuse"
- Comparison content: "Self-hosted Node-RED vs. FlowFuse Cloud"
- Webinars: live product walkthroughs, use-case demos
- Gated content: whitepapers, architecture guides, integration checklists
- Newsletter content nurturing existing contacts
- Customer stories with technical depth

**Primary distribution:** Email nurture, LinkedIn, webinar channels, gated landing pages

---

### BOFU — Bottom of Funnel (Decision)

BOFU content reaches people who are ready to act. The goal is to remove friction and convert interest into a trial signup, demo request, or contact sales submission.

**Audience:** Decision-makers and champions ready to evaluate FlowFuse. Often at [Stage 3–4](/handbook/marketing/messaging/#prospect-stages-node-red-awareness) or actively comparing vendors.

**Characteristics:**
- Product and proof-of-value focused
- Emphasizes ROI, security, and enterprise readiness
- Social proof is critical: customer stories, reviews, certifications
- Clear, direct CTAs (sign up, book a demo, contact sales)

**Examples for FlowFuse:**
- Customer stories with measurable outcomes (cost savings, deployment time, scale)
- Pricing page content and comparison tables
- Free trial onboarding content
- Demo request pages
- G2 review campaigns
- Technical security and compliance documentation for procurement

**Primary distribution:** Direct sales follow-up, email sequences, website landing pages, paid retargeting

---

## Content Mix Target

A healthy content programme maintains output across all three stages. As a rule of thumb:

| Stage | Share of content output | Primary goal |
|-------|------------------------|--------------|
| TOFU  | ~60%                   | Organic reach and brand awareness |
| MOFU  | ~30%                   | Lead capture and consideration |
| BOFU  | ~10%                   | Pipeline conversion |

TOFU dominates because it compounds over time through SEO and community sharing. BOFU content is fewer pieces but high-impact.

## Content Planning & Scheduling

### The content calendar

All content — blog posts, webinars, social posts, gated assets, emails — is tracked in the [Asana content calendar](https://app.asana.com/1/1213818720452348/project/1214069528696432/calendar/1214069573785519). This is the single source of truth for what is planned, who owns it, and when it publishes.

Every piece of content should have:
- An assigned owner
- A scheduled publish date
- A funnel stage (TOFU / MOFU / BOFU)
- A content type (blog, social, webinar, email, etc.)

### Two-week rolling schedule

We plan at least **two weeks ahead** at all times. This means:
- All content items for the next 14 days have an owner, a publish date, and any required assets (blog tiles, social copy, visuals) in progress.
- Social posts for the next two weeks are approved and scheduled in the [HubSpot social calendar](https://app-eu1.hubspot.com/social/26586079/manage/calendar).
- Any content that requires external input (e.g., engineers writing a tutorial, sales providing customer approval for a story) is flagged with a deadline far enough in advance to not block publishing.

The two-week buffer exists to avoid reactive, last-minute content that lacks context or quality review.

### Weekly review

Every week, during the [marketing team meeting](/handbook/marketing/#marketing-kpi), the team reviews the content calendar for the next two weeks:

1. **Status check**: Confirm every item in the next 14 days has an owner and is on track.
2. **Gap identification**: Flag any gaps in coverage — missing publish days, imbalanced funnel stages, or channels that have gone quiet.
3. **Prioritization**: Surface any timely or high-priority items (product releases, industry news, campaigns) that should be added or bumped.
4. **Social confirmation**: Verify that social posts for the coming week are scheduled in HubSpot.

This review is a standing agenda item, not a special meeting. It takes 10–15 minutes.

### Feedback loop with content producers

Content producers include the marketing team, engineers writing technical tutorials, the podcast team, sales writing customer insights, and external collaborators. The feedback loop ensures their work is integrated into the plan and that performance data flows back to them.

**Proposing new content:**
1. Open an issue in the [marketing repository](https://github.com/FlowFuse/marketing/issues) or add it directly to the Asana content calendar.
2. Tag the Product Marketing Manager for review.
3. Include: topic idea, target audience, funnel stage, and any deadline.

**Approval and scheduling:**
- The Product Marketing Manager reviews proposals against the current content mix and strategic priorities.
- Approved items are added to the Asana content calendar with a target date.
- The proposer is responsible for drafting; marketing handles SEO review, scheduling, and distribution.

**Performance feedback:**
- After a piece of content has been live for at least four weeks, performance data (organic traffic, social engagement, lead conversions) is reviewed in the [monthly marketing performance review](/handbook/marketing/how-we-work/#monthly-marketing-performance-tracking).
- Insights from that review — which topics resonated, which formats performed, which stage needs more content — feed directly into the next planning cycle.
- If a content producer's piece performs particularly well or poorly, the Product Marketing Manager shares that context with them to improve future contributions.

### Social posts

Social posts are part of the content calendar, not a separate track. Each blog, webinar, or product update on the calendar should have associated social posts planned at the same time.

For social-specific planning, posting hours, format guidelines, and platform distribution rules, see the [Social Media handbook](/handbook/marketing/social-media/#planning-and-posting).
